Theorem: Matrix Entrywise Addition Properties

Let \(\mathcal{M}_S(m, n)\) be an \(m \times n\) matrix space over \(S\) over an algebraic structure \((S, \circ)\). Let \(A, B \in \mathcal{M}_S(m, n)\). Let \(A + B\) be the matrix entrywise sum of \(A\) and \(B\). Then

  • \(+\) is closed on \(\mathcal{M}_S(m, n) \iff \circ\) is closed on \((S, \circ)\)
  • \(+\) is associative on \(\mathcal{M}_S(m, n) \iff \circ\) is associative on \((S, \circ)\)
  • \(+\) is commutative on \(\mathcal{M}_S(m, n) \iff \circ\) is commutative on \((S, \circ)\)

Proof: Closure

Let \([a]_{mn}, [b]_{mn} \in \mathcal{M}_S(m, n)\) and let \([c]_{mn} = [a]_{mn} + [b]_{mn}\).$$\forall i \in [1 \,.\,.\, m], j \in [1 \,.\,.\, n] : c(i, j) = a(i, j) \circ b(i, j).$$\((S, \circ)\) is closed \(\iff c(i, j) \in S\). But, \([c]_{mn}\) has the same dimensions as \([a]_{mn}\) and \([b]_{mn}\), from the definition of matrix entrywise addition, so \([c]_{mn} \in \mathcal{M}_S(m,n)\). Therefore, \((\mathcal{M}_S(m, n), +)\) is closed. The argument reverses, hence the result.
