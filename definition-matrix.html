Definition Of Matrix

Let \(m, n \in \mathbb{Z}_+\) and let \(S\) be a set. An \(m \times n\) matrix over \(S\) is a mapping from the cartesian product of two integer intervals \(\left[{1 \,.\,.\, m}\right] \times \left[{1 \,.\,.\, n}\right]\) into \(S\).

Dimensions

Let \(\left[{a}\right]_{mn}\) be an \(m \times n\) matrix. The parameters \(m\) and \(n\) are called the dimensions of \(\left[{a}\right]_{mn}\).

Elements

Let \(\textbf{A}\) be an \(m \times n\) matrix over \(S\). The \(m \times n\) elements of \(S\) that form \(\textbf{A}\) are called elements of \(\textbf{A}\).

Indices

Let \(\textbf{A}\) be an \(m \times n\) matrix and let \(a(i, j)\) be an element of \(\textbf{A}\). Then \(i\) and \(j\) are called the indices of \(a(i, j)\).

Rows

Let \(\textbf{A}\) be an \(m \times n\) matrix. \(\forall i \in \left[{1 \,.\,.\, m}\right]\), the rows of \(\textbf{A}\) are the ordered \(n\)‑tuples \(r_i = \left({a(i, 1), a(i, 2), \ldots, a(i, n)}\right)\), where \(r_i\) is the \(i^{th}\) row of \(\textbf{A}\).

Columns

Let \(\textbf{B}\) be an \(m \times n\) matrix. \(\forall j \in \left[{1 \,.\,.\, n}\right]\), the columns of \(\textbf{B}\) are the ordered \(m\)‑tuples \(c_j = \left({b(1, j), b(2, j), \ldots, b(m, j)}\right)\), where \(c_j\) is the \(j^{th}\) column \(\textbf{B}\).

Underlying Structure

Let \(\textbf{A}\) be a matrix over \(S\). \(S\) is called the underlying set of \(\textbf{A}\). If \(S\) is the underlying set of an algebraic structure, the structure \(\left({S, \circ_1, \circ_2, \ldots, \circ_n}\right)\) is called the underlying structure of \(\textbf{A}\).

Square Matrix

A square matrix is a matrix with the same number of rows and columns. A square matrix is denoted \(\left[{a}\right]_n\).

Principal Diagonal

Let \(\textbf{A} = \left[{a}\right]_n\) be a square matrix. The elements \(a(j, j) : j \in \left[{1 \,.\,.\, n}\right]\) make up what is called the principal diagonal of \(\textbf{A}\). The elements of the principal diagonal are called diagonal elements.

Zero Rows And Zero Columns

Let \(\textbf{A} = \left[{a}\right]_{mn}\) be an \(m \times n\) matrix whose underlying structure is a ring or field. If a row or column of \(\textbf{A}\) has only zeroes from the ring or field, the row or column is called a zero row or a zero column, respectively.
